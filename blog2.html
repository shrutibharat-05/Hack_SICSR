Last year, I had to cut down an old mango tree in our yard due to structural damage to our house. While it seemed like the only solution, it left a hole in my heart. Over time, I realized how interconnected we are with the trees around us. The air seemed less fresh, and the birds no longer visited my garden.

What I Learned:

  • Consider alternatives like trimming or shifting your construction plans before cutting down a tree.
  • If cutting is inevitable, plant at least two trees to compensate for the loss.
  • Respect the age and significance of the trees around you; they are living beings with a history.

Though I had to cut down the tree, I’ve since planted five new ones in my neighborhood. The lesson I learned is that we should avoid cutting unless absolutely necessary and always work toward replenishing what’s lost.
