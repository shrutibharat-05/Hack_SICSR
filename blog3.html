Inspired by Miyawaki’s technique, I decided to turn a small part of my backyard into a mini forest. With a mix of native trees, bushes, and flowering plants, I’ve been able to create a dense, self-sustaining ecosystem in less than two years. It’s now home to various birds, butterflies, and even squirrels.

Tips for Creating Your Own Mini Forest:

  • Pick a variety of native plants and trees that support biodiversity.
  • Plant them densely to create a natural, forest-like growth pattern.
  • Let nature take its course – avoid unnecessary pruning or maintenance. Let it grow wild!

This small forest in my backyard is now my sanctuary. It's proof that even small urban spaces can contribute to afforestation and biodiversity.
